# Hydraulic and Water Engineering Writing Base

Apply this layer when polishing or drafting water-conservancy, hydrology, water
resources, river engineering, flood control, drainage, sediment transport,
hydraulic-structure, urban flooding, water-environment, or water-governance
texts.

This file is a general water-engineering base. It should raise the engineering
quality, data discipline, and anti-AI naturalness of papers, reports, and course
deliverables. It must not turn one previous topic into a default template.
Object-specific checks run only when the current materials actually contain that
object.

## Core Stance

- Start from the water problem, then narrow to the study object, mechanism,
  method, scenario, or engineering decision.
- Tie significance to a concrete use: flood-risk assessment, reservoir or gate
  operation, drainage design, river regulation, water allocation, ecological
  flow, water-quality control, sediment management, model reliability, or
  project governance.
- Prefer mechanism-and-condition claims. State what changes, under which flow,
  rainfall, boundary condition, terrain, roughness, operation, or scenario.
- Name the scale: experiment, section, reach, river network, catchment, basin,
  region, design event, return period, model grid, or time series.
- Keep uncertainty visible. For model, evaluation, or planning work, mention
  calibration, validation, sensitivity, parameter uncertainty, data quality,
  forcing uncertainty, or transfer limits when relevant.

## Chinese Water-Writing Rhythm

For Chinese water-engineering papers and reports, use this practical rhythm:

`object or site -> pressure or contradiction -> data/method/index system ->
result or spatial difference -> engineering use or boundary`.

- Object first: name the basin, reach, river network, lake, reservoir, drainage
  area, project, model, dataset, evaluation object, or management unit before
  broad policy value.
- Pressure: specify runoff change, flood risk, pollutant input, water shortage,
  sediment imbalance, channel change, regulation conflict, monitoring gap,
  construction/operation risk, governance fragmentation, or data limitation.
- Method: keep method nouns concrete, such as hydrological calculation,
  hydraulic modelling, water-quality evaluation, index system, entropy weight,
  fuzzy comprehensive evaluation, set-pair analysis, AHP, scenario simulation,
  sensitivity analysis, scheme comparison, or performance assessment.
- Results: report status, grade, ranking, range, trend, spatial cluster,
  hotspot, threshold, short-board factor, subsystem imbalance, or uncertainty
  before interpretation.
- Engineering use: say who can use the result and for which decision, such as
  planning screening, parameter selection, operation scheduling, risk control,
  project comparison, quality control, monitoring layout, maintenance, or later
  verification.

## Local Paper Exemplar Learning

When the user provides a local folder of water-conservancy papers, treat it as
the style and structure corpus for the final output. Read enough papers to learn
the target writing habits before polishing or drafting. Do not rely only on this
generic base.

- Build a short style profile from the corpus: chapter order, paragraph length,
  opening pattern, method-description granularity, table/figure explanation,
  result wording, conclusion pattern, and reference style.
- Extract reusable rhetorical moves, not claims: how papers introduce the object,
  narrow the problem, define indicators or methods, report grades/trends/spatial
  differences, and turn results into engineering use.
- Prefer corpus-consistent Chinese wording when it is clearer than generic
  journal prose. The final text should sound like a real water-engineering paper
  or report, not like a universal academic template.
- Keep evidence discipline strict. The corpus teaches writing form and technical
  rhythm; it does not authorize copying wording, importing unsupported data, or
  transferring conclusions to the user's project.
- When a corpus and a teacher/template requirement conflict, preserve the
  user's task book, template, and source data first; use the corpus to improve
  expression, density, and argument flow.

## Corpus-Derived Normative Expression Profile

The local 21-paper corpus under `论文搭建` shows a consistent water-engineering
register. Use these observations when the user asks for paper-level or
normative expression:

- The dominant action words are not ornate. They are working verbs such as
  evaluation, analysis, governance, risk, model, simulation, construction of an
  index system, weighting, grade, distribution, trend, scenario, and
  applicability. In the extracted sample, the strongest signals were `评价`,
  `分析`, `治理`, `风险`, `模型`, `构建`, `权重`, `等级`, `空间分布`, `趋势`, and
  `情景`.
- Abstracts usually start by naming an object and pressure, then state the
  method or index system, then summarize the main result and engineering use.
  Avoid opening with broad value claims before the water object is clear.
- Methods are written as reproducible paths: data source, study object,
  indicator/model, weight or scenario setting, calculation/evaluation rule, and
  output table or spatial result. A method sentence should answer "what data",
  "what method", and "what output".
- Results avoid vague adjectives. Prefer grade, range, spatial difference,
  trend, hotspot, constraint factor, ranking, subsystem difference, or scenario
  comparison.
- Conclusions do not merely praise significance. They state what the result can
  inform: planning, governance, scheme selection, monitoring, operation,
  maintenance, parameter choice, risk control, or later verification.
- The corpus style is formal but plain. It raises the output by tightening
  evidence and boundaries, not by making sentences more grand.

## Anti-AI Prose Checks

For Chinese drafts, reduce AI-like regularity after the facts and data are
stable.

- Avoid empty outline language in the submitted body: `主线`, `论证主线`,
  `技术路线`, `逻辑链条`, `研究框架`, `整体思路`, `本报告先`, `后文按照`,
  `避免把`, `若继续完善`, and similar phrases. Keep a required heading if the
  template demands it, but fill it with inputs, methods, parameters, outputs,
  and checks.
- Convert overview sentences into engineering content: object, data source,
  formula, model setting, table result, parameter basis, design consequence, or
  boundary.
- Prefer work verbs that name a real action: 评价, 治理, 分析, 构建, 控制, 协同,
  修复, 识别, 集成, 比选, 校核, 率定, 验证, 模拟, 复核.
- Avoid slogan-like verbs such as 赋能, 打造, 全面提升, 深度优化, 持续发力 unless
  the source is an official-policy document and that register is required.
- Reduce repeated openings such as `本文通过`, `本文构建`, `首先`, `其次`,
  `综上`. Let the object, condition, result, or comparison carry the transition.

## Evidence And Data Discipline

Before sentence-level polish, check whether each important paragraph has:

- object: basin, reach, section, model, dataset, indicator, parameter, project,
  table, figure, scenario, operation rule, or management unit;
- basis: measured data, task-book value, code/standard, formula, model setting,
  literature support, engineering analogy, or labelled assumption;
- consequence: result interpretation, scheme choice, parameter setting, risk
  level, operation implication, design/control action, or management advice;
- boundary: missing input, data period, calibration range, scenario condition,
  model assumption, regional transfer limit, or verification item.

If a paragraph only describes the author's writing plan, flag it as a structure
problem. Do not solve it with smoother wording.

## Tables, Calculations, And Data Closure

Tables are evidence, not decoration. A table that affects a conclusion must have
an auditable path from source to number to judgment.

- Recompute table-driven conclusions with deterministic tools when arithmetic
  matters. This includes weighted scores, water depth, elevation difference,
  runoff reduction, pollutant load, storage, flow velocity, risk index, and
  repeated formula outputs.
- State the source or rule for weights, scores, parameters, thresholds, classes,
  and calculated values. Acceptable bases include measured data, task-book data,
  design code, method formula, literature source, expert/teacher requirement,
  engineering analogy, or labelled assumption.
- Show the calculation chain when a number supports a decision:
  `formula -> substitution -> result -> judgment`. One full worked example plus
  a verified table is acceptable for repeated rows.
- Keep units, datum, object, period, scenario, and calculation basis explicit.
  Different values may coexist when their bases differ; unexplained drift for
  the same object and basis is a defect.
- After changing a value, search the whole artifact for old values and update
  related prose, tables, figures, conclusions, and verification notes together.

## Structure By Document Type

- Introduction or background: open with a real pressure point or knowledge/design
  gap. Avoid textbook openings.
- Methods: make data sources, boundary conditions, assumptions, parameter
  sources, calibration/validation, indicator construction, and scenario settings
  reproducible.
- Results: report quantities with units, ranges, baselines, and uncertainty when
  available. Separate result description from explanation.
- Discussion: explain why the result occurs, where it applies, and where it may
  fail. Avoid turning one case into a universal rule.
- Conclusion: state the main engineering takeaway, use value, and transfer limit.
- Reports/coursework: preserve the user's task book, teacher sample, school
  template, and formatting requirements. Use higher-level paper style only to
  improve clarity, evidence density, and boundaries.

## General Report Audit Gate

Before final delivery of Chinese water-engineering reports or coursework:

- Fix arithmetic, table-data mismatch, unsupported parameter drift, and missing
  sources before cosmetic polishing.
- Check that terms with multiple meanings carry their basis, such as water level
  datum, return period, design condition, freeboard/safety margin, risk grade,
  evaluation period, or scenario name.
- Make every strong recommendation traceable to data, formula, standard,
  model result, table, figure, literature support, or labelled assumption.
- Normalize references, especially standards and local task materials, using one
  consistent citation style.
- Keep teacher-facing prompts, self-check notes, and process language out of the
  submitted body. Convert them into formal basis, calculation, assumption, or
  limitation statements.
- If an official school or teacher Word template is available, final Word
  delivery must preserve the native template cover and document structure. Use
  the template as the base document, or merge the body into it, instead of
  recreating the cover by hand. Verify cover fields, section/page settings,
  headings, updateable TOC fields, headers/footers, and required formatting
  after the merge.
- For Word/PDF/Markdown outputs, reopen or parse the actual artifact and check
  that key numbers remain present, old values are absent, table/paragraph counts
  are plausible, and there is no mojibake or unexpected `?` corruption.

## Preferred Wording

- Use restrained verbs: indicates, constrains, modifies, propagates, attenuates,
  amplifies, delays, redistributes, couples, bounds, supports, or improves under
  specified conditions.
- Avoid inflated verbs unless the evidence supports them: revolutionizes,
  comprehensively solves, proves, fully reveals, or universally applies.
- Replace vague nouns such as effect, change, optimization, and improvement with
  water quantities when possible: discharge, water level, inundation depth, flow
  velocity, shear stress, storage, runoff coefficient, pollutant load, sediment
  load, dissolved oxygen, risk grade, or prediction interval.
- Translate Chinese engineering-purpose claims cautiously. `可为...提供依据`
  usually means can inform, provides a basis for, or offers technical support
  for.

## Claim Checks

Before final polish, check strong claims against these questions:

- What water process, engineering decision, or management decision does it affect?
- What spatial and temporal scale supports it?
- Which data source, scenario, boundary condition, calibration range, or method
  assumption limits it?
- Is the claim transferable to another basin, river, project, event, or model
  setting?
- Can the reader identify the data source, calculation path, table/figure anchor,
  or citation that supports the claim?
