# Hydraulic and Water Engineering Expression

Apply this layer when polishing hydraulic engineering, hydrology, water
resources, river engineering, flood control, drainage, sediment transport,
hydraulic structures, eco-hydraulics, or broad civil/environmental engineering
work. Also apply it to Chinese water-conservancy course reports and drafts on
urban rivers, ecological revetments, stormwater runoff, soil and water
conservation, river-network connectivity, shoreline resources, water ecological
health, river pollution control, and water-resource governance.

Recent water-engineering articles in journals such as Nature Water, Journal of
Hydrology, Advances in Water Resources, Water Resources Research, Nature
Communications, and ASCE water journals tend to reward prose that is specific
about process, scale, boundary conditions, and operational relevance. For
water-conservancy coursework and design reports, use this top-journal style to
raise the ceiling of clarity, boundary awareness, terminology precision, and
data support; keep the chapter structure, formatting, task requirements, and
teacher samples governed by the course-design materials.

## Argument stance

- Lead with the engineering or hydrological problem, then narrow to the
  mechanism, model, experiment, site, or scenario studied.
- Tie significance to a concrete use: flood-risk assessment, hydraulic
  structure operation, reservoir regulation, river restoration, drainage design,
  water allocation, ecological flow, sediment management, or model reliability.
- Prefer mechanism-and-condition claims over broad novelty claims. Say what
  changed, under which flow, rainfall, boundary, terrain, roughness, or
  operating condition.
- State scale clearly: laboratory flume, reach, catchment, basin, regional
  model, design event, return period, or scenario set.
- Keep uncertainty visible. When the evidence is model-based, name calibration,
  validation, sensitivity, parameter uncertainty, forcing uncertainty, or
  extrapolation limits as appropriate.

## Chinese hydraulic-writing patterns

When polishing Chinese drafts or Chinese-to-English translations learned from
local water-engineering papers, preserve the practical research rhythm:

`object/site -> pressure or contradiction -> method/index system -> result
state or spatial difference -> engineering use`.

- Object/site first: name the reach, basin, urban river type, revetment project,
  shoreline segment, construction project, lake system, or drainage/stormwater
  scenario before broad policy value.
- Pressure or contradiction: specify urbanization, runoff growth, non-point
  pollution, river channelization, poor bank material, complex flow conditions,
  ecological degradation, flood risk, fragmented governance, or maintenance and
  quality-control difficulty.
- Method/index system: keep method nouns concrete: entropy weight, fuzzy
  comprehensive evaluation, set-pair analysis, AHP-grey evaluation, BMP
  screening, pressure-state-response model, index system, technical integration,
  suitability analysis, construction quality control, or scheme comparison.
- Results: report status, grade, ranking, gradient, spatial cluster, hotspot,
  short-board constraint, subsystem imbalance, or superposed degradation before
  interpretation.
- Engineering use: state who can use the result and for what decision: planning
  screening, revetment selection, construction quality control, pollution
  treatment performance assessment, shoreline supervision, ecological
  restoration, operation and maintenance, or later hydraulic/scour/stability
  checks.

## Avoid meta-outline wording in Chinese course reports

For Chinese hydraulic course reports, do not overuse abstract organization
phrases such as `主线`, `论证主线`, `技术路线`, `逻辑链条`, `研究框架`, or
`整体思路`. They often read as AI-like because they describe the manuscript's
outline rather than the engineering work. Use them only when the school template
requires a method-flow section.

- Do not merely make meta-outline sentences smoother. If a sentence such as
  "全文论证主线为...", "技术路线为...", "本报告先...再...",
  "后文按照...展开", or "避免把...处理成..." only exposes the author's plan,
  delete it or return it to the structural layer. It should not become polished
  body text.
- Convert necessary overview sentences into engineering judgments or chapter
  duties. For example, replace a planning sentence with a result-boundary
  statement such as "MJ6两岸堤顶高程均高于课程设计阶段控制高程，断面设计先处理
  岸脚防冲、坡面反滤排水和结构稳定；生态布置不得挤占行洪断面，也不能削弱
  边坡稳定。"
- For ecological revetment passages, treat catalogue sentences like `防冲、排水、
  护坡、生态布置` or slogan sentences like `基于上述...统筹...遵循...原则` as
  likely AI-like. Rewrite them as a local sequence: name the hydrodynamic or
  seepage pressure, state the design action, then give the safety boundary for
  vegetation or landscape measures. Avoid quotation-mark principles such as
  `安全优先、生态兼容` unless the task book or teacher sample uses that exact frame.
- If an overview is required, state what each design unit does rather than how
  the report is ordered: control-elevation checking determines whether crest
  raising is needed; scheme comparison selects the revetment type; typical
  section design fixes toe protection, filter drainage, slope cover and plant
  zoning dimensions.
- Template-required method sections are exceptions. If the user's task book,
  school template, or teacher sample explicitly requires an independent section
  named `技术路线`, `研究思路`, `研究主线`, or similar, preserve that heading and
  its place in the structure. The anti-AI rule applies to the content, not to
  required template labels: fill the section with concrete inputs, parameters,
  formulas/models, output tables, drawing deliverables, and verification
  checkpoints instead of empty process connectors.
- Prefer hydraulic action verbs in Chinese drafts: 判断, 计算, 识别, 初选, 比选,
  确定, 说明, 复核.
- When the sentence describes flow, sediment, or bank-response mechanisms, use
  harder physical verbs when the local evidence supports them: 束窄过流断面,
  抬高沿程水位, 激压坡脚, 水力剪切, 淘刷岸脚, 促发冲刷, 挟沙输移, 淤堵孔隙,
  渗流顶托, and 诱发坡面浅层滑移. Avoid using these as decorative jargon; each
  phrase should point to a visible section change, flood process, seepage path,
  sediment process, or verification item.
- If the word must remain, the next sentence must convert it into auditable
  engineering content: data source, formula, substitution, parameter source,
  table, or follow-up verification.

## Course-report structural steering

For Chinese water-conservancy course reports, polishing must not compensate for
a weak generation direction. Before sentence-level edits, check whether the
paragraph is driven by a chapter duty:

- object: reach, section, water level, elevation, bank toe, filter layer,
  revetment structure, plant zone, construction or maintenance object;
- basis: task-book datum, formula, standard/code, engineering analogy, or
  labelled course-design assumption;
- consequence: scheme choice, section dimension, table result, design annotation,
  construction sequence, maintenance item, or later verification;
- boundary: missing design discharge, measured velocity, bed gradation, normal
  water level, shear-strength parameter, or other input that limits the design.

If a paragraph only explains the author's intended order, argument design, or
style goal, flag it as a structure problem instead of polishing it. The fix
belongs in `section_blueprints.md`, `writing_rationale_matrix.md`, or the local
chapter, not in cosmetic wording.

## Section-level emphasis

- Introduction: avoid textbook openings such as "water is essential". Open with
  a pressure point in engineering practice or water-system behaviour, then state
  the unresolved process, data, or design limitation.
- Methods: make boundary conditions, governing assumptions, parameter sources,
  mesh/grid choices, calibration data, validation ranges, and scenario settings
  reproducible.
- Results: report hydraulic or hydrological quantities with units, ranges, and
  comparison baselines. Separate observed/modelled behaviour from interpretation.
- Discussion: explain why the result occurs, where it can be applied, and where
  it may fail. Do not turn a calibrated case result into a universal design rule.
- Conclusion: state the engineering takeaway and the limit of transferability in
  the same breath.

## Hard audit gate for Chinese hydraulic course-design reports

For Chinese water-conservancy course designs, especially ecological revetment and typical-section reports, run this gate before stylistic polishing. Treat failures here as engineering defects, not wording defects.

- Recompute every table-driven conclusion. For weighted scoring, parse the stated weights and scores, multiply row by row, compare against the table and prose, and update all occurrences. Do not preserve a value only because it appears in the source. If arithmetic cannot be verified from the artifact, flag it explicitly before polishing.
- Collapse duplicated calculation sections. If a later chapter repeats earlier elevation arithmetic, replace the repeated calculation with a cross-reference plus design consequence, for example: the control crest result moves the chapter focus from crest raising to flow-section influence, toe scour, filter drainage, and slope stability.
- Check cover-page completion. Detect blank fields such as student ID, student name, class, instructor, and date. If the data are not available, ask for them or flag the blanks; do not leave a supposedly final Word report with empty identity fields.
- Check data-scope consistency, not mechanical sameness. The same object under the same design stage and calculation basis must keep the same value and term across prose, tables, conclusions, and submitted artifacts. Different objects or bases may validly differ, such as freeboard relative to design flood level versus freeboard relative to control crest, but the basis must be labelled. For structures such as a three-dimensional vegetation net or geocell, require a corresponding role in the relevant section parameters or slope-stability prose only when that structure is part of the designed load-bearing or soil-retention system, not merely a plant suggestion.
- Defend empirical parameters. Embedment depth, stone size, geotextile mass, cushion thickness, cover-soil thickness, slope ratio, and platform width need a source, course-design assumption, or explicit verification boundary. For mountain streams or cobble-bed reaches, toe embedment such as 0.80 m must be paired with later local-scour verification and possible deepening or front apron/riprap protection.
- Add construction-direction details when they affect performance. For geotextiles and filter layers, state overlap width, laying direction, and sequence, for example upstream fabric overlapping downstream fabric and bottom-up slope installation where appropriate.
- Normalize engineering terminology. Replace loose headings such as `水利理由` with `技术可行性分析`, `合理性论证`, or another standard engineering title that matches the section job.
- Audit references as report content. Enforce one GB/T 7714-style punctuation pattern throughout: standard number/title/type, publication place, publisher, year, and consistent half-width punctuation plus spaces where used.
- Inspect table pagination when producing Word/PDF. If a table splits after edits, repeat the header and add a continued-table label where the school format requires it.

The required audit order is: fatal arithmetic and data closure -> cover fields -> data-scope consistency -> engineering parameter boundaries -> reference and layout polish -> student-voice humanization.
## Engineering Depth Failure Patterns In Ecological Revetment Reports

Use this layer when a Chinese ecological revetment or typical-section report is
already structurally complete but still feels thin under stricter engineering
review. Diagnose the cause of the weakness first, then add only the evidence or
boundary the local materials can support.

- Parameter-without-mechanism: stone size, gabion height, embedment depth,
  geotextile mass, cushion thickness, or planting-soil thickness are easy to
  read as "picked by feel" when the paragraph names the value but not the
  hydraulic action it resists. Fix by linking the value to scour, uplift,
  seepage, retention, drainage, or construction control. If measured inputs are
  missing, use a labelled course-design assumption or later verification item
  instead of inventing field data.
- Calculation-gap masking: a report may state "data are missing" and then jump
  to a final parameter. This creates distrust because the reader cannot tell
  whether the value is a conservative preliminary selection or an unsupported
  design result. When possible, add one bounded preliminary check, such as an
  assumed flow-velocity range or empirical sizing relation. Mark the check as
  preliminary and keep the missing inputs visible.
- Drawable-geometry gap: typical-section text fails when a CAD drafter cannot
  draw the structure from the prose. For layered or modular revetments, close
  the geometry through layer count, layer height, width, embedment, top/bottom
  elevations, and the connection form. If the task does not require that detail,
  note it as a drawing-detail boundary rather than expanding the whole chapter.
- Arithmetic-trust gap: prose can demand visible calculations, but language
  models are unreliable calculators. For elevations, water depths, freeboard,
  weighted scores, Manning/Chezy/Isbash-style substitutions, particle sizing,
  or any repeated numeric table, use a deterministic tool such as Python,
  spreadsheet formulas, or structured table parsing to compute the numbers.
  The writing layer should explain the formula, substitution, result, and
  design judgment after tool verification, not mentally calculate complex
  arithmetic.
- Boundary-overload gap: missing-input boundaries can become defensive
  repetition if every technical section repeats the same disclaimer. State the
  main calculation/data boundary once in the overview or calculation-boundary
  section and once in the later review/check section. Middle design chapters
  should normally proceed with confident course-design wording such as
  "preliminarily selected for course-design purposes" and reserve repeated
  caveats for genuinely new missing inputs.
- Reference-side omission gap: when one bank or side is said to "refer to" the
  controlling side, the model may compress it into a vague sentence and lose
  quantities. If the report needs drawings, quantities, or symmetric section
  understanding, still write the reference side's elevations, layer sequence,
  and geometry explicitly. Do not use "same as the right/left bank" to skip
  physical closure.
- Filter-function gap: geotextile and cushion layers are often described as a
  generic "filter-drainage layer". This blurs the engineering roles. Separate
  soil retention, permeability, clogging resistance, drainage, leveling, and
  fabric protection. If gradation data are absent, require later opening-size
  and soil-gradation checks without fabricating O95, d85, or other values.
- Terminology-basis gap: words such as freeboard, margin, safety allowance, and
  "余高" become confusing when the reference level changes. Use terms that name
  the basis: margin relative to design flood level, margin relative to control
  crest elevation, or construction allowance.
- Scoring-subjectivity gap: scheme-comparison scores are weak when the table
  gives numbers but not the engineering reason behind high or low scores. Add a
  short scoring rule and one or two reasoned contrasts tied to flood action,
  toe stability, construction difficulty, maintenance, ecology, or river type.
- Standard-currency gap: reference lists look stronger when current standards
  are checked, but unverified "latest" standards are worse than omissions. Add
  newer national, industry, local, or teacher-specified guidance only when it is
  present in the materials or has been verified.
- Construction-transfer gap: design parameters do not automatically become
  buildable details. When performance depends on installation, add observable
  controls such as gabion filling and closure, settlement accommodation,
  geotextile laying direction, crest anchorage, overlap contact, vegetation-mat
  fixing, or access-feature edge treatment.
- Scope-quantity gap: "left bank refers to right bank" and local access features
  can make quantities ambiguous. Clarify whether a side is fully counted,
  locally preserved, or only shown as a structural reference; when steps or
  access features cross the slope, state how they meet the toe, filter, and
  drainage system.

## Calculation presentation and formatting contract

For Chinese hydraulic course-design reports, key calculations must be visible, not compressed into conclusions. A calculation is key when it supports a design judgment, table score, elevation relation, section dimension, material parameter, construction boundary, or final conclusion.

- Present key calculations as `formula -> substitution -> result -> design judgment`. Do not only state the final value when the value affects a recommendation.
- Show one worked substitution for every table-driven method, including weighted scheme comparison. If several rows use the same method, one full worked example plus a verified table is acceptable, but all table values must be recomputed.
- Keep different calculation bases explicit. For example, freeboard relative to design flood level and freeboard relative to control crest are both valid if labelled by formula.
- For Word deliverables, important formulas should be standalone display equations rendered as native Word equations from LaTeX/OMML. Do not leave important formulas as plain inline text when a rendered equation is feasible.
- For LaTeX deliverables, use display equations or aligned equations and keep the same formula/substitution/result chain.
- If a formula cannot be rendered as a Word equation in the current environment, flag the limitation and keep the LaTeX source visible instead of silently degrading to unclear prose.
- Formatting is user-specified first. When the user gives font, heading, table, caption, spacing, margin, formula, or reference requirements, follow them exactly. Do not invent custom styles. If a requirement is missing, preserve the existing template or ask for the missing item before changing visible formatting.
## Chinese coursework student voice pass

For Chinese water-conservancy course reports, after the structural and data
checks pass, reduce AI-like polish without weakening the engineering register:

- Cut unnecessary long sentences into one judgment sentence plus one basis or
  boundary sentence.
- Vary paragraph openings and reduce repeated `本报告...`, `本文...`,
  `综上可知...`, and `通过计算可知...` patterns.
- Turn list-like `第一、第二、第三、第四` prose into direct statements about
  control elevation, section parameter, scheme choice, construction sequence,
  or follow-up verification.
- Keep a restrained undergraduate-coursework tone: specific, slightly plain,
  and tied to the local data. Avoid both slogan-style official prose and
  over-refined journal prose.
- Never remove required template text, headings, captions, references, table
  labels, formulas, units, or numeric conclusions during this pass.

Run an artifact read-back after editing Chinese files and check for mojibake,
question-mark corruption, and unchanged key numbers.

## Chinese coursework anti-regression checklist

For Chinese water-conservancy course reports, use this final audit after the
structural, data, and wording passes:

- Template first: if the user supplies a model assignment or sample report,
  learn its structure, calculation order, table design, wording density,
  reference format, and section-by-section method before drafting. The sample
  controls the report's shape unless it conflicts with the user's task book or
  data.
- No agent-process prose in the submitted body. Search for and remove phrases
  such as `本报告先`, `后文`, `论证重点`, `技术路线`, `主线`, `展开`, `避免把`,
  `若继续完善`, `自查`, and `还需补足`. Replace them with engineering facts:
  site condition, control value, formula, design consequence, construction
  control, maintenance item, or verification boundary.
- Preserve required template headings even when they contain words normally
  flagged as meta-outline language, such as `技术路线` or `研究思路`. The repair is
  to make the paragraph substantive: input data, calculation model, parameter
  source, output table/figure, and verification route. Do not delete a required
  heading merely to reduce AI traces.
- Teacher-facing prompts, correction notes, and checklist language are not
  report prose. Sentences like `涉及权重、评分和参数取值的表格，均需...`,
  `避免表格数据脱离计算过程`, `应优先补足...`, or `若继续完善...` must be
  converted into formal statements of adopted basis, formula, assumption,
  standard source, or exact chapter/table location. Do not leave them as
  "helpful reminders" in the submitted body.
- Re-run a data-closure pass after every numeric change. Recompute and update
  all related values in body text, tables, plant zoning, conclusions,
  Then search for the old value globally.
- Check typical-section geometry explicitly: bed elevation, toe bottom, toe
  top, embedment depth, structure height, design flood level, control crest,
  berm, layer thicknesses, and path width must all close arithmetically.
- Check spatial zoning after elevation edits. Near-water zone, fluctuation
  zone, normal bank-slope zone, berm/crest zone, and maintenance path should
  connect through explicit elevation ranges when the report has those values.
- Remove repeated disclaimers or duplicated "actual engineering should verify"
  sentences when adjacent paragraphs already state the same boundary. Keep one
  concise limitation tied to the missing input or verification item.
- Require scheme-comparison closure: explain weight basis, score scale,
  weighted-sum formula, and one calculation example; then ensure the conclusion
  no longer contains self-correction notes about missing weights or scores.
- Use water-engineering terminology accurately: filter fabric/geotextile =
  soil retention and fine-particle control; gravel cushion/transition layer =
  drainage, leveling, and filter-layer protection; gabion/stone toe =
  local anti-scour and structural continuity.
- References are part of the audit. For Chinese standards, prefer complete
  entries with issuing organization, standard number, title `[S]`, publication
  place, publisher, and year. For course task books, school formatting
  requirements, teacher handouts, templates, and local design briefs, also
  complete the responsible organization, title, document type such as `[R]`,
  and year; keep normal spacing after punctuation.
- Verify the actual Word/PDF/Markdown artifact after editing Chinese content:
  file opens or parses, paragraph/table counts are stable, required numbers
  remain present, old values are absent, banned process phrases are absent, and
  there is no mojibake or unexpected `?` corruption.

## Tables, weights, and calculation trace

For Chinese water-conservancy course reports and hydraulic engineering drafts,
tables are not only formatting objects. A table that carries a decision must
have an auditable path from source to number to design consequence.

- Do not polish unsupported table numbers as if they were established facts.
  If a weight, score, parameter, or calculated value lacks a source, formula, or
  judgment rule, flag the gap and ask for or add a labelled assumption.
- For scheme-comparison weights, state the method or basis: AHP pairwise
  comparison, entropy weight, equal weights, code/rubric priority, teacher/task
  requirement, literature source, or course-design assumption. If weights are
  assumed, explain the engineering order behind them.
- For scoring tables, define the scale before the table. State whether larger
  scores are better and how qualitative items such as construction difficulty,
  maintenance convenience, landscape effect, or river-type suitability are
  converted into scores.
- For weighted scores, include the formula near the table, for example
  "composite score = sum(weight x score)", and show at least one substitution
  for the recommended scheme. The table may summarize all schemes, but prose
  must show how the preferred option was obtained.
- For hydraulic or structural parameters, state the value source: task-book
  data, design code, calculation, empirical preliminary selection, site
  constraint, or later verification item. This applies to water level,
  freeboard, flood depth, slope ratio, toe width, embedment depth, stone size,
  geotextile mass, filter/cushion thickness, planting soil thickness, and path
  width.
- When data are insufficient for exact design, use bounded wording such as
  "preliminary estimate for course-design purposes" and list the missing inputs
  needed for engineering design.

## Data closure and data-scope consistency

Before final polish, run a data-closure pass whenever the draft contains
calculated values, section geometry, elevations, layer thicknesses, weighted
scores, plant zones, tables, or Word/PDF deliverables.
Treat an unexplained drift in the same value as a reasoning defect; treat a valid difference in object, basis, or design stage as acceptable when the label is clear.

- Recompute weighted scores from the stated weights and scores. If the table,
  formula expansion, and prose disagree, repair the local table and prose.
- Check geometric closure in typical sections. Elevations, embedment depth,
  layer thickness, structure height, bed elevation, toe-top elevation, and
  toe-bottom elevation must satisfy the stated arithmetic.
- After changing any numeric parameter, search the whole artifact for the old
  value. Update the introduction, calculation paragraph, parameter table,
  plant-zone table, conclusion, and verification list together.
- Tie ecological or plant zones to usable engineering elevations when available:
  bed elevation, toe top, flood fluctuation zone, design flood level, berm,
  crest, or maintenance path. Avoid vague zones when the report has exact
  elevation intervals.
- Separate layer functions. Geotextile or filter fabric mainly provides
  retention/soil protection; gravel cushion or transition layers mainly provide
  drainage, leveling, and protection of the filter layer.
- For ecological revetments with gabions, geotextiles, soil cover, or planted
  slopes, distinguish ordinary water-level fluctuation from unsteady-flow or
  rapid-drawdown conditions. When the site is a steep mountain stream,
  reservoir/backwater reach, or flood hydrograph with fast recession, flag the
  possibility that high pore water in the bank drains outward after external
  water level falls, creating seepage pressure, filter-drainage demand, and
  shallow cover-soil sliding risk. Do not invent a stability calculation, but
  require the prose to name rapid drawdown as a later filter drainage, seepage,
  scour, or slope-stability check when the inputs are unavailable.
- Remove instruction-like self-checking prose from the final body. Replace it
  with formal wording that states the adopted formula, assumption, standard
  basis, or the chapter where the calculation is provided.
- For generated Word/PDF outputs, verify the actual artifact after editing:
  the file exists, opens/parses, old values are absent, new values occur where
  expected, and obvious encoding damage such as `????` is absent.

## Preferred wording habits

- Use restrained verbs: indicates, constrains, modifies, propagates, attenuates,
  amplifies, delays, redistributes, couples, bounds, or improves under specified
  conditions.
- Avoid inflated verbs unless the evidence supports them: revolutionizes,
  comprehensively solves, proves, fully reveals, or universally applies.
- Avoid vague nouns such as effect, change, optimization, and improvement when a
  hydraulic quantity can be named: water level, discharge, velocity, shear
  stress, inundation depth, travel time, storage, sediment load, dissolved oxygen,
  runoff coefficient, or prediction interval.
- Keep Chinese-to-English translations domain-specific: "水动力条件" is usually
  hydraulic conditions or hydrodynamic conditions; "调度" is operation or
  regulation depending on context; "冲淤" is erosion-deposition or scour and
  deposition; "水沙过程" is flow-sediment process.
- For Chinese drafts, prefer work verbs that name a real research or engineering
  action: 评价, 治理, 分析, 构建, 控制, 协同, 修复, 识别, 集成, 比选, 校核.
  Avoid slogan-like verbs such as 赋能, 打造, 全面提升, 深度优化, or 持续发力
  unless the user's source text is an official-policy document.
- Translate Chinese engineering-purpose claims cautiously. "可为...提供依据"
  is usually "can inform", "provides a basis for", or "offers technical support
  for", not "proves" or "guarantees".
- Replace vague Chinese claims such as "效果显著" with the measured or observed
  carrier: health grade, runoff reduction, pollutant load, treatment
  performance index, construction quality, vegetation recovery, NPP change,
  risk level, or spatial distribution.

## Claim checks

Before final polish, check every strong claim against these questions:

- What physical process or engineering decision does this claim affect?
- At what spatial and temporal scale is it supported?
- Which boundary condition, calibration range, or scenario limits the claim?
- Is the result transferable to another river, catchment, structure, or event,
  or only suggestive beyond the study case?
- For Chinese water-conservancy course reports, does each recommendation name a
  decision target such as scheme selection, section design, material control,
  construction sequence, inspection, flood-season maintenance, or follow-up
  hydraulic/scour/stability verification?
- For every table that affects a recommendation, can the reader identify the
  data source, formula or scoring rule, substitution/calculation process, and
  resulting design consequence?
